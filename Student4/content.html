Volleyball Techniques

Volleyball is a sport that requires a combination of physical abilities and technical skills.
It is a game that involves two teams of six players each, trying to score points by hitting a ball over a net and onto the opponent's court, while preventing the opposing team.
some of the techniques used in volleyball are shown below

Spike block

The block is a defensive technique used to prevent the ball from crossing the net. A good block requires proper timing, positioning, and coordination with the rest of the team.

Serving

The serve is the first contact made in a volleyball game.
A good serve can put pressure on the opposing team and make it harder for them to score points.

Passing

Passing is the second contact made in a volleyball game.
It involves using your forearms to receive the ball and pass it to a teammate. Good passing is essential for setting up successful attacks.

Setting

Setting is the third contact made in a volleyball game.
It involves using your fingertips to direct the ball to a teammate for a hit. A good set can create opportunities for powerful attacks.

Hiting

Volleyball hitting consists of spiking a set ball, usually coming from the setter. The volleyball approach and spike includes the approach, jump, arm-swing, hand contact, and landing. All front line attackers should be working to spike the ball hard.

Digging

Digging is known as defend in normal terms. Defending is a vital technique for players to hold on to leads they already achieved.
To dig a volleyball up in defense you keep an attacked ball by the opposing team off your court floor by extending your arms and hands under the ball to deflect it back in the air before it hits the floor.
